Urban Navigation and Safety

Overview

Our project involves designing and developing a sustainable Urban Navigation and Safety systems covering 1500 square kilometers smart city. The objective is to create a technologically advanced, sustainable, and citizen-centric urban environment navigation and safety systems that enhances quality of life, fosters economic growth, and serves as a model for sustainable urban development.

Key Features:

  • Leverage advanced algorithms for efficient Navigation and Safety.
  • Use data-driven insights for optimizing traffic and public weather.
  • Integrate modern technologies to ensure sustainability and citizen engagement.

By combining efficient algorithms and data structures, we aim to develop solutions that are innovative and impactful.

Problem Space and How It Was Arrived At

Urban navigation and safety face challenges like dynamic traffic, GPS inaccuracies, and infrastructure limitations, complicating efficient and safe travel. Pedestrian and cyclist interactions, multimodal transport, and unpredictable road user behaviors add complexity. Environmental hazards, crime risks, and accident prevention require robust systems. Data accuracy, IoT integration, and computational constraints limit technological solutions. Legal, equity, and urban planning gaps further hinder progress. Emerging solutions include AI for traffic prediction, connected vehicles , advanced sensors, and crowdsourced data for real-time insights. Addressing these challenges requires collaboration across urban planning, technology, and policy to create safer, more efficient urban transportation systems..
